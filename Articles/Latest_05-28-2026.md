### 1. KPMG Deploys Claude to 276,000 Employees Across 138 Countries

**Source:** [Build Fast With AI – AI News Today May 28, 2026](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-may-28-2026)

In one of the largest enterprise AI rollouts ever announced, KPMG confirmed the deployment of Anthropic's Claude assistant to its entire global workforce of 276,000 people spanning 138 countries. The move signals a massive vote of confidence in Claude for professional services workflows and underscores that the real AI race is no longer just about model benchmarks — it's about who controls enterprise deployment at scale.

### 2. OpenAI Launches "DeployCo" — A $4 Billion AI Consulting Subsidiary

**Source:** [Build Fast With AI – AI News Today May 28, 2026](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-may-28-2026)

OpenAI officially launched the OpenAI Deployment Company ("DeployCo"), a majority-owned standalone subsidiary backed by over $4 billion from investors including TPG, Goldman Sachs, McKinsey, Bain Capital, and Capgemini. DeployCo embeds Forward Deployed Engineers directly inside client organizations to accelerate AI adoption, putting OpenAI in direct competition with traditional management consultancies and systems integrators.

### 3. Cohere Acquires Aleph Alpha to Build $20B Sovereign AI Giant

**Source:** [Build Fast With AI – AI News Today May 28, 2026](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-may-28-2026)

Cohere announced the acquisition of German AI startup Aleph Alpha, creating a transatlantic sovereign AI company valued at approximately $20 billion. The deal is seen as a strategic counter to US hyperscaler dominance, positioning the combined entity as the leading independent AI provider for governments and enterprises in Europe and North America that require data sovereignty and regulatory compliance.

### 4. Apple WWDC to Unveil Siri 2.0 with Google Gemini Integration

**Source:** [LLM Stats – AI News May 2026](https://llm-stats.com/llm-updates)

Ahead of Apple's WWDC (June 8–12 at Apple Park), reports confirm that the keynote will feature a comprehensive Siri overhaul powered in part by Google's Gemini models. Apple has registered a new subdomain, genai.apple.com, pointing to the revamped assistant infrastructure. The move marks a significant shift in Apple's AI strategy, choosing to partner with Google rather than build large foundation models entirely in-house.

### 5. Canada Rules OpenAI Violated Federal Privacy Laws

**Source:** [Build Fast With AI – AI News Today May 28, 2026](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-may-28-2026)

Canada's Office of the Privacy Commissioner concluded that OpenAI violated the country's federal privacy legislation, making Canada one of the first jurisdictions to issue a formal legal ruling against a major AI lab over data handling practices. The ruling adds regulatory pressure on AI companies globally and may accelerate calls for similar investigations in the EU and US.
