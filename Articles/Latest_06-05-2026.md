## 2. NVIDIA Releases Nemotron 3 Ultra: 550B Open-Weights Model for Long-Running Agents

**Source:** [MarkTechPost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/04/nvidia-ai-releases-nemotron-3-ultra-an-open-550b-mixture-of-experts-hybrid-mamba-transformer-for-long-running-agents/) | [Artificial Analysis](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/nvidia-nemotron-3-ultra-launch-announced) | [Decrypt](https://decrypt.co/369689/nvidia-open-ai-model-nemotron-3-ultra)

NVIDIA launched Nemotron 3 Ultra at Computex 2026, a 550B-parameter (55B active) open-weights model built on a hybrid Mamba-Transformer Mixture-of-Experts architecture. Designed for long-running agentic tasks, it supports a 1 million token context window and achieves over 300 tokens per second. It is now the highest-scoring US open-weights model on intelligence benchmarks, though it still trails China's Kimi K2.6. The model is available on HuggingFace, OpenRouter, and NVIDIA NIM under a commercial-friendly license.

## 3. Trump Signs Executive Order Requiring Voluntary 30-Day Pre-Release Review of Frontier AI Models

**Source:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/02/trump-executive-order-ai.html) | [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/06/02/trump-ai-executive-order-sets-30-day-frontier-model-review/5250322) | [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/02/nx-s1-5844347/ai-safety-trump-executive-order)

President Trump signed "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security" on June 2, 2026, directing AI developers to voluntarily submit frontier models to government review 30 days before public release. The Treasury Department, NSA, and CISA are tasked with defining what qualifies as a "frontier model." The order is explicitly non-mandatory — it does not create a licensing or permitting requirement — but represents a significant pivot toward federal AI oversight. A prior May draft had proposed a 90-day window; the reduction to 30 days was Trump's key modification.
