## 2. US Export Ban on Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 Enters Day 9

**Source:** [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access) | [Time](https://time.com/article/2026/06/13/anthropic-fable-mythos-ban-US-security/) | [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/19/us-export-ban-on-anthropics-ai-models-further-strains-alliances)

The US Commerce Department's June 12 export control directive — the first ever applied directly to an AI model rather than hardware — has kept Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 offline for nine days. The ban requires Anthropic to suspend all access for any foreign national, anywhere in the world. The government cited a jailbreaking technique as the trigger. As of June 21, Anthropic's Managing Director of International said they are "very confident that in the coming days, the models will become available again," the most specific positive signal yet suggesting active negotiations with the Commerce Department are producing progress.

## 4. OpenAI's AI Chemist Achieves Real Drug Discovery Breakthrough

**Source:** [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-new-capabilities-to-gpt-rosalind/) | [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/318618/20260618/ai-drug-discovery-chemistry-hits-wet-lab-gpt-54-boosts-chan-lam-yields-10080-reactions.htm)

OpenAI published research on June 17 showing that GPT-5.4, connected to Molecule.one's Maria high-throughput lab system, ran 10,080 reactions and found a validated improvement to Chan-Lam coupling — a copper-catalyzed reaction used to form carbon-nitrogen bonds in small-molecule drug discovery. Under optimized conditions, average estimated product yield rose from 16.6% to 25.2%, and the share of reactions clearing 30% yield jumped from 15.6% to 37.5%. This marks the first publicly documented instance of a frontier AI model acting as a near-autonomous agent in a real wet-lab chemistry workflow.

## 5. FERC Orders Grid Operators to Fast-Track AI Data Center Power Connections

**Source:** [FERC](https://www.ferc.gov/news-events/news/ferc-directs-nations-largest-grid-operator-create-new-rules-embrace-innovation-and) | [Gizmodo](https://gizmodo.com/a-federal-regulator-wants-to-fast-track-ai-data-centers-onto-the-power-grid-2000773803)

On June 18, the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission unanimously issued six tailored "show-cause" orders to the nation's largest regional grid operators (PJM, MISO, SPP, CAISO, ISO-NE, NYISO), requiring them to either justify their current interconnection rules or propose reforms to allow AI data centers and other large-load customers to connect to the power grid faster. The operators have 60 days to respond. Rather than a uniform national rule, FERC is taking a region-by-region approach, recognizing differences in market design, geography, and stakeholder processes.
