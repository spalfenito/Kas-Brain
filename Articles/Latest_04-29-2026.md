## Story 1: NVIDIA Launches Ising — World's First Open AI Models for Quantum Computing

**Source:** [NVIDIA Newsroom](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/nvidia-launches-ising-the-worlds-first-open-ai-models-to-accelerate-the-path-to-useful-quantum-computers)

NVIDIA unveiled **Ising**, the world's first family of open-source AI models purpose-built to accelerate quantum computing. The models target the two biggest barriers in practical quantum computing: error correction and processor calibration. Ising delivers quantum error-correction decoding that is up to **2.5x faster and 3x more accurate** than traditional approaches. Early adopters include Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory, Harvard, Lawrence Berkeley National Lab, and IQM Quantum Computers. The release sent quantum computing stocks surging, with IonQ gaining over 20%.

## Story 2: OpenAI Launches GPT-Rosalind, Its First Life Sciences AI Model

**Source:** [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-rosalind/) | [FierceBiotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/openai-launches-biotech-specific-ai-model-gpt-rosalind)

OpenAI shipped **GPT-Rosalind** on April 16, 2026 — its first domain-specific model targeting drug discovery, biochemistry, genomics, and protein engineering. Named after DNA pioneer Rosalind Franklin, the model is optimized for scientific workflows with deep tool-use capabilities. Access is currently gated via a vetted trusted-access program with early partners including **Amgen, Moderna, Thermo Fisher Scientific, the Allen Institute, and Dyno Therapeutics**. Analysts view it as OpenAI's first major push beyond general-purpose AI into specialized vertical markets.

## Story 4: Google Releases Gemma 4 — Most Capable Open Model Family of 2026

**Source:** [Google Blog](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/gemma-4/) | [Google DeepMind](https://deepmind.google/models/gemma/gemma-4/)

Google launched **Gemma 4** on April 2, 2026, a new family of open models released under the Apache 2.0 license and built for advanced reasoning, coding, function calling, and multimodal understanding. The lineup spans four sizes — 2.3B, 4.5B, 26B MoE, and 31B Dense — making frontier-grade AI accessible on consumer hardware. Gemma 4's 26B model, combined with Google's new **TurboQuant** KV-cache compression algorithm (presented at ICLR 2026), can run with a full 262K context window on a **single RTX 4090** GPU — a significant milestone for local AI deployment.
