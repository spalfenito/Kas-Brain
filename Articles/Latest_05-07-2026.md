## 5. AI System "Synthegy" Lets Chemists Plan Molecular Synthesis in Plain English

**Source:** [ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/05/260504023844.htm) | [EPFL](https://actu.epfl.ch/news/ai-helps-chemists-design-molecules-step-by-step/) | [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-00240-5)

Researchers at EPFL, led by Philippe Schwaller, have developed **Synthegy** — a framework that pairs traditional retrosynthesis search algorithms with large language models to let chemists direct complex molecular synthesis using natural language. Chemists describe their synthesis goals in plain text; Synthegy converts candidate routes into text, has an LLM score each against the stated strategy, and surfaces the best options with written explanations. In a double-blind study with 36 chemists providing 368 evaluations, human assessments agreed with Synthegy's rankings 71.2% of the time. The system can flag inefficient protecting-group steps, evaluate reaction feasibility, and elucidate mechanisms by decomposing reactions into elementary moves — all without replacing the chemist, but amplifying their decision-making.
