## 1. OpenAI Announces GPT-5.5

**Source:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/23/openai-announces-latest-artificial-intelligence-model.html)

OpenAI unveiled GPT-5.5, its latest frontier model offering major improvements in coding, computer use, and deep research capabilities. The model is rolling out to paid subscribers — Plus, Pro, Business, and Enterprise — via ChatGPT and the Codex coding assistant. GPT-5.5 is positioned as a key step toward a unified AI "super app" that integrates chat, coding tools, and browser-based capabilities into a single interface.

## 2. DeepSeek Releases V4 Preview — Closes Gap With Frontier Models

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/24/deepseek-previews-new-ai-model-that-closes-the-gap-with-frontier-models/) | [MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/04/24/1136422/why-deepseeks-v4-matters/) | [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/24/deepseek-v4-llm-preview-open-source-ai-competition-china.html)

Chinese AI lab DeepSeek released a preview of its highly anticipated V4 model in two variants: **DeepSeek-V4-Pro** (1.6 trillion parameters, 49B activated) and **DeepSeek-V4-Flash** (284B parameters, 13B activated), both supporting a 1-million-token context window. The models use a hybrid Mixture-of-Experts architecture with a novel Compressed Sparse Attention mechanism for long-context efficiency. V4-Pro outperforms GPT-5.2 and Gemini-3.0-Pro on reasoning benchmarks and leads all open-source models in agentic coding — at a fraction of the cost, priced at $1.74/million input tokens for the Pro tier.

## 3. Meta Debuts Muse Spark — First Model From Superintelligence Labs

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/08/meta-debuts-the-muse-spark-model-in-a-ground-up-overhaul-of-its-ai/) | [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/08/meta-muse-alexandr-wang) | [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-08/meta-debuts-first-ai-model-from-prized-superintelligence-group)

Meta launched **Muse Spark** (internally codenamed "Avocado"), the first model from its Superintelligence Labs led by Chief AI Officer Alexandr Wang, whom Meta recruited after a $14.3 billion deal to acquire a 49% stake in Scale AI. Built over nine months, Muse Spark accepts voice, text, and image inputs and excels at visual STEM tasks, multimodal reasoning, and health information processing — though Meta acknowledges a gap versus leading labs in coding. The model is free to use on the web and the Meta AI app, and notably breaks from Meta's open-source Llama playbook as a closed-source release.

## 4. AI Software Platforms Market to Hit $106.92B in 2026

**Source:** [GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/27/3281500/28124/en/artificial-intelligence-software-platforms-global-market-report-2026-google-microsoft-aws-tencent-and-ibm-led-the-79-38-billion-market-in-2025.html)

A new global market report projects the AI software platforms sector will grow from **$79.38 billion in 2025 to $106.92 billion in 2026** — a roughly 35% year-over-year increase. Growth is driven by surging machine learning adoption, data-driven decision-making, advances in natural language processing, and the continued expansion of cloud computing infrastructure. Google, Microsoft, AWS, Tencent, and IBM were identified as the top five companies leading the market in 2025.

## 5. MIT Develops Faster Method to Estimate AI Energy Consumption

**Source:** [MIT News](https://news.mit.edu/2026/faster-way-to-estimate-ai-power-consumption-0427)

MIT researchers unveiled **EnergAIzer**, a technique that can rapidly predict how much power an AI workload will consume when run on a specific processor — without needing to actually run the full workload. The method could help data center operators and AI developers make more informed decisions about hardware selection and model efficiency, improving the sustainability of AI workloads at scale. The research comes amid growing scrutiny of AI's rising energy footprint as frontier model training and inference demands continue to climb.
