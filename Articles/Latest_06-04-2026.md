## 1. Microsoft Launches MAI-Thinking-1 at Build 2026

**Source:** [CNBC — Microsoft unveils new AI models to lessen reliance on OpenAI](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/02/microsoft-unveils-new-ai-models-lessen-reliance-on-openai-lower-costs.html)

At its annual Build developer conference in San Francisco, Microsoft unveiled seven new in-house AI models under the MAI (Microsoft AI) brand, headlined by MAI-Thinking-1 — its first flagship reasoning model built entirely without distillation from OpenAI or any third-party model. MAI-Thinking-1 features 35 billion active parameters and a 256,000-token context window. In blind evaluations, it matched Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.6 on SWE Bench Pro coding benchmarks and outperformed Claude Sonnet 4.6 in user preference tests. The model is available in private preview via Microsoft Foundry, signaling a strategic push for Microsoft to control its full AI stack independent of OpenAI.

## 2. DeepSeek Eyes $7.4 Billion in First-Ever Funding Round

**Source:** [Bloomberg — DeepSeek Close to Sealing $7 Billion Funding in Historic AI Deal](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-06-03/deepseek-close-to-sealing-7-billion-funding-in-historic-ai-deal)

Chinese AI startup DeepSeek — best known for its open-source V3 and R1 models — is preparing to raise approximately $7.4 billion (50 billion yuan) in its first-ever external funding round, targeting a valuation of up to $59 billion. Key investors include Tencent Holdings, battery giant CATL, and a government-backed National AI Industry Investment Fund. Founder Liang Wenfeng is personally contributing roughly 40% of the total. The deal would rank among the largest private tech financings in China and marks a dramatic shift for a company that previously bootstrapped its rise to global prominence without venture capital. The round is expected to close within weeks.

## 3. NVIDIA Releases Nemotron 3 Ultra — 550B Parameter Open-Weights Model

**Source:** [Artificial Analysis — Nemotron 3 Ultra announced: high-speed, leading US open weights intelligence](https://artificialanalysis.ai/articles/nvidia-nemotron-3-ultra-launch-announced)

NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang unveiled Nemotron 3 Ultra at Computex 2026, and the model officially shipped on June 4. With 550 billion total parameters (55 billion active per token via a hybrid Mamba-Transformer mixture-of-experts architecture), it claims the top spot on the Intelligence Index among US open-weights models with a score of 48. The MoE design enables 300+ tokens/second throughput at 5x faster inference and 30% lower cost than comparable open-weight alternatives. The model is available on Hugging Face, ModelScope, OpenRouter, and as an NVIDIA NIM microservice, making it the most powerful freely available US-origin model to date.

## 4. White House Issues AI Innovation and Security Executive Order

**Source:** [The White House — Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/)

President Trump signed a wide-ranging executive order on June 2 targeting frontier AI models and national cybersecurity. Key provisions include a voluntary pre-release review program allowing the government to test powerful AI models up to 30 days before public launch, an AI cybersecurity clearinghouse (coordinated with CISA and NSA) to identify and patch software vulnerabilities at scale, and classified benchmarking processes to flag models with advanced cyber capabilities. The order explicitly prohibits mandatory government licensing or pre-clearance for AI models, and directs the DOJ to prioritize enforcement against criminal AI use. The order is broadly seen as balancing innovation promotion with light-touch national security guardrails.

## 5. AI Agents and Enterprise Automation Emerge as 2026's Defining Trend

**Source:** [AI Startup Edge — AI News June 2026: Biggest Updates and Trends](https://aistartupedge.com/ai-news-june-2026/)

Across the AI industry in June 2026, autonomous AI agents have emerged as the dominant enterprise theme — surpassing chatbot-style AI tools in investment and deployment. Unlike traditional prompt-response systems, agents execute multi-step tasks with minimal human input, automating entire workflows end-to-end. Major players including Anyscale (which launched a new Azure-native unified compute platform for the full AI lifecycle) are racing to make agent deployment easier and cheaper. Analysts note that the winners in 2026 are not those with the highest benchmark scores, but companies building repeatable, production-ready AI workflows. The broader race has expanded beyond models to encompass chips, energy infrastructure, capital, and regulatory positioning.
