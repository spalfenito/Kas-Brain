## 3. Disney + OpenAI: $1B Investment Seals Landmark Sora Character Licensing Deal

**Source:** [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/disney-sora-agreement/) | [The Walt Disney Company](https://thewaltdisneycompany.com/news/disney-openai-sora-agreement/)

Disney has signed a landmark three-year agreement with OpenAI, making it the first major content licensing partner on **Sora**, OpenAI's generative video platform. Disney will invest **$1 billion** in OpenAI equity and receive warrants for additional shares. The deal enables Sora and ChatGPT Images to generate fan-prompted short videos featuring more than **200 characters** from Disney, Marvel, Pixar, and Star Wars. Disney will also deploy OpenAI's APIs internally — including for Disney+ product development — and roll out ChatGPT access across its workforce. The company's internal AI coordination body, the **Office of Technology Enablement**, now operates at full scale with over 100 experts spanning Studios, Parks, and Streaming.

## 4. Cadence + NVIDIA Expand Partnership to Close the Robotics Sim-to-Real Gap

**Source:** [Cadence Newsroom](https://www.cadence.com/en_US/home/company/newsroom/press-releases/pr/2026/cadence-and-nvidia-expand-partnership-to-reinvent-engineering.html) | [BusinessWire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260415332711/en/Cadence-and-NVIDIA-Expand-Partnership-to-Reinvent-Engineering-for-the-Age-of-AI-and-Accelerated-Computing)

Announced at **CadenceLIVE Silicon Valley 2026**, Cadence and NVIDIA are deepening their collaboration to tackle the persistent **sim-to-real gap** in robotics. The integrated workflow combines Cadence's high-fidelity multiphysics simulation engines with NVIDIA's **Isaac** robotics libraries, **Cosmos** open-world models, and **Omniverse** digital twin infrastructure. The pipeline spans virtual training in Isaac Sim and Isaac Lab through large-scale scenario testing, with deployment on NVIDIA Jetson edge AI systems. The partnership also targets AI factory design, using the NVIDIA Omniverse DSX Blueprint to simulate next-generation Vera Rubin and Grace Blackwell AI training clusters.

## 5. Amazon Commits Up to $25B More to Anthropic in Expanded AI Infrastructure Deal

**Source:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/20/amazon-invest-up-to-25-billion-in-anthropic-part-of-ai-infrastructure.html)

Amazon has agreed to invest **up to $25 billion** in Anthropic — on top of $8 billion already committed — as part of a sweeping AI infrastructure deal. In return, Anthropic is committing to spend **over $100 billion on Amazon Web Services** over the next decade, cementing AWS as the primary cloud backbone for Anthropic's model training and inference at scale. The deal cements Amazon's position as the dominant cloud infrastructure partner for one of the most closely watched frontier AI labs, as competition with Microsoft-OpenAI and Google DeepMind intensifies.
