## 3. Elon Musk's Multi-Billion Dollar OpenAI Lawsuit Thrown Out

**Source:** [NPR — Jury dismisses all claims in Elon Musk's lawsuit against OpenAI](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/18/nx-s1-5822366/musk-altman-openai-jury-verdict-claims-dismissed)

A California jury deliberated for less than two hours before unanimously rejecting Elon Musk's entire lawsuit against OpenAI CEO Sam Altman and co-founder Greg Brockman. Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers of the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California concurred, dismissing the case on statute-of-limitations grounds — ruling that Musk waited too long to file when he launched the suit in 2024. The jury never reached the merits of Musk's claims that Altman and Brockman breached a "charitable trust" by pivoting OpenAI away from its founding nonprofit mission. The verdict clears a major legal cloud over OpenAI as the company prepares for a highly anticipated IPO expected later this year.

## 5. Hybrid Light-Matter Particle Could Transform AI Computing

**Source:** [ScienceDaily — Artificial Intelligence News](https://www.sciencedaily.com/news/computers_math/artificial_intelligence/)

Researchers at the University of Pennsylvania have engineered a hybrid light-matter particle — a quantum quasiparticle called a polariton — that could dramatically accelerate AI computing while consuming far less energy than current silicon-based systems. The breakthrough leverages the speed of photons with the interactivity of electrons, potentially enabling AI inference at optical speeds. Separately, NASA is testing a next-generation space computer chip designed to give deep-space spacecraft the ability to perform AI-driven autonomous decision-making independently, without waiting for instructions from Earth — a communication lag that can stretch to 20+ minutes at interplanetary distances.
