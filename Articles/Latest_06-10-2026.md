## 2. China Unveils $295 Billion Plan for Nationwide AI Data Center Network

**Source:** [Tech Startups](https://techstartups.com/2026/06/09/china-unveils-295-billion-plan-to-build-a-nationwide-ai-data-center-network-and-reduce-reliance-on-u-s-chips/)

China is preparing to spend roughly 2 trillion yuan ($295 billion) over the next five years to build an interconnected network of AI data centers across the country, led by agencies including the National Development and Reform Commission and operated largely by China Mobile and China Telecom. The plan aims to source at least 80% of AI chips and related technology domestically (primarily from Huawei), reducing reliance on Nvidia and AMD. Including related power-grid upgrades, total associated investment could exceed 5 trillion yuan.

## 3. Microsoft's Open Source Tools Hacked to Steal AI Developers' Passwords

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/08/microsofts-open-source-tools-were-hacked-to-steal-passwords-of-ai-developers/)

Microsoft cut off access to dozens of open source projects on GitHub after discovering that hackers had injected password-stealing malware into the code. Many affected projects relate to Azure and developer tools used with AI coding assistants such as Claude Code, the Gemini CLI, and VS Code — allowing attackers to steal credentials when developers opened compromised tools. Microsoft says it has temporarily removed some repositories pending investigation, restoring others after review. This appears to be the second such incident in recent weeks, following a May breach of Microsoft's Durable Task project.

## 5. SpaceX Reveals Orbital AI Data Center Plans Ahead of Massive IPO

**Source:** [CoinCentral](https://coincentral.com/spacex-reveals-orbital-ai-data-center-plans-ahead-of-1-75-trillion-ipo/)

The vision follows SpaceX's $250 billion acquisition of xAI earlier this year, with Anthropic and Google reportedly already paying for compute capacity from xAI's data centers.
