## 2. Apple's iOS 27 Will Let Users Choose Their Own AI Model

**Source:** [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-05/ios-27-features-apple-plans-to-let-users-swap-models-across-apple-intelligence) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/05/apple-plans-to-make-ios-27-a-choose-your-own-adventure-of-ai-models/)

Apple plans to open up **Apple Intelligence** to third-party AI providers in iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27 this fall. Internally dubbed "Extensions," the system will allow users to route Siri, Writing Tools, Image Playground, and other Apple Intelligence features through providers like Google Gemini and Anthropic Claude — selected directly from the Settings app. Users will even be able to assign different Siri voices to different AI providers. Formal developer documentation is expected at WWDC 2026 (June 8–12).

## 3. AWS and OpenAI Expand Partnership: GPT Models, Codex & Managed Agents on Amazon Bedrock

**Source:** [AWS](https://aws.amazon.com/about-aws/whats-new/2026/04/bedrock-openai-models-codex-managed-agents/) | [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/openai-on-aws/)

Amazon Web Services and OpenAI announced an expanded partnership bringing three new offerings to Amazon Bedrock in limited preview: **OpenAI Models on Bedrock** (including GPT-5.5 and GPT-5.4) available through Bedrock's existing APIs; **Codex on Bedrock**, bringing OpenAI's coding agent into AWS environments via CLI, desktop app, and VS Code; and **Amazon Bedrock Managed Agents powered by OpenAI**, enabling fast deployment of production-ready AI agents. All usage counts toward existing AWS cloud spending commitments and sits alongside models from Anthropic, Meta, Mistral, and others under unified security and governance controls.

## 4. Meta's Muse Spark: First Model from Meta Superintelligence Labs

**Source:** [Meta AI Blog](https://ai.meta.com/blog/introducing-muse-spark-msl/) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/08/meta-debuts-the-muse-spark-model-in-a-ground-up-overhaul-of-its-ai/)

Meta unveiled **Muse Spark**, the debut model from its newly formed Meta Superintelligence Labs, on April 8, 2026. The natively multimodal reasoning model supports visual chain-of-thought, tool use, and multi-agent orchestration — and can reach the same capabilities as its predecessor Llama 4 Maverick with over 10× less compute. Built after Meta invested $14.3 billion in Scale AI for a 49% stake and brought on co-founder Alexandr Wang to lead the labs, Muse Spark is live on the web and Meta AI app, with rollout to Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, and Ray-Ban Meta glasses coming in the weeks ahead.
