## 3. Google's TurboQuant Achieves 6x KV Cache Compression at ICLR 2026

**Source:** [Google Research Blog — TurboQuant: Redefining AI efficiency with extreme compression](https://research.google/blog/turboquant-redefining-ai-efficiency-with-extreme-compression/) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/25/google-turboquant-ai-memory-compression-silicon-valley-pied-piper/)

Google's TurboQuant algorithm, presented at ICLR 2026, compresses the key-value (KV) cache in large language models down to as few as 3 bits per value — achieving up to 6x memory reduction with near-zero accuracy loss and no model retraining required. For a 70B-parameter model serving 128K-token contexts, KV cache alone can consume 40GB+ of GPU VRAM; TurboQuant effectively makes long-context inference viable on far less hardware. Independent developers have already built open-source implementations in PyTorch and llama.cpp from the paper's math. The breakthrough has major implications for on-device AI and data center operating costs.

Snap CEO Evan Spiegel announced the elimination of approximately 1,000 positions and the closure of over 300 open roles, explicitly attributing the decision to rapid advances in AI that allow smaller teams to match the output of larger ones. Simultaneously, labor tensions around AI-driven cuts escalated across multiple industries: Wikipedia editors organized a strike over Wikimedia layoffs, Amazon workers reportedly gamed the company's internal AI ranking system, and Chinese courts began enforcing a legal framework that bars employers from using AI advancement as sole justification for terminations.
