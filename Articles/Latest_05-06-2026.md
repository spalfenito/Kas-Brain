## 2. Anthropic, Goldman Sachs & Blackstone Launch $1.5 Billion AI Enterprise Venture

**Source:** [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/04/anthropic-goldman-blackstone-ai-venture.html)

Anthropic is finalizing a ~$1.5 billion joint venture with Blackstone, Goldman Sachs, Hellman & Friedman, and other Wall Street firms to deploy Claude-powered AI tools across private-equity-owned companies. Unlike traditional consulting, the venture will embed engineers inside portfolio companies to redesign workflows. The initial rollout targets industries including healthcare, manufacturing, financial services, retail, and real estate. Rival OpenAI is reportedly pursuing a near-identical structure with TPG and Bain Capital.

## 3. Google DeepMind Workers in U.K. Vote to Unionize Over Military AI Contracts

**Source:** [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/05/05/google-deepmind-unionize-vote-military-ai-contracts-internal-backlash-pentagon-deal-israeli-defense-forces/)

Google DeepMind employees in the United Kingdom voted to unionize amid internal backlash over the company's Pentagon AI contract. More than 600 Google employees have signed an open letter opposing the military deal, with several speaking out publicly. The move echoes the 2018 "Project Maven" controversy but analysts note employee leverage has diminished significantly since then. The unionization push marks a rare instance of organized labor action inside a major AI lab.

## 5. Snap Cuts 1,000 Jobs — 16% of Workforce — Citing AI Efficiency

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/15/snap-is-cutting-1000-jobs-16-of-its-workforce/)

Snap CEO Evan Spiegel announced the layoff of approximately 1,000 full-time employees — 16% of the company's global workforce — attributing the cuts to "rapid advancements in artificial intelligence" that allow smaller teams to achieve the same output. AI now handles roughly 65% of Snap's coding work. The restructuring is expected to reduce Snap's annualized cost base by more than $500 million by the second half of 2026, with severance packages including four months of pay and equity vesting for U.S. employees.
