**Source:** [NxCode](https://www.nxcode.io/resources/news/deepseek-v4-release-specs-benchmarks-2026) | [Lushbinary Developer Guide](https://lushbinary.com/blog/deepseek-v4-developer-guide-trillion-parameter-moe-engram/) | [FindSkill.ai](https://findskill.ai/blog/deepseek-v4-release-date-specs/)

Built on Huawei Ascend chips rather than Nvidia hardware, V4 achieves 81% on SWE-bench and 97% needle-in-a-haystack accuracy at 1M-token scale, challenging the dominance of Western AI labs.

## 3. Neuro-Symbolic AI Cuts Energy Use by 100x While Boosting Accuracy

**Source:** [ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2026/04/260405003952.htm) | [SciTechDaily](https://scitechdaily.com/?p=514311) | [Engineering & Technology Magazine](https://eandt.theiet.org/2026/04/07/ai-system-could-cut-energy-use-100-times-researchers-say)

Researchers at Tufts University, led by Professor Matthias Scheutz, unveiled a **neuro-symbolic AI** system that combines neural networks with symbolic reasoning to dramatically cut energy consumption. In trials using the Tower of Hanoi puzzle, the new approach achieved a 95% success rate versus 34% for standard vision-language-action models — while using only 1% of the training energy and 5% of operational energy. Training time dropped from 36+ hours to just 34 minutes. The research will be presented at the International Conference on Robotics and Automation in Vienna in May 2026.

## 4. Utah Becomes First State to Allow AI to Renew Drug Prescriptions

**Source:** [Axios Salt Lake City](https://www.axios.com/local/salt-lake-city/2026/01/07/utah-ai-drug-prescriptions-doctronic) | [The Hill](https://thehill.com/policy/healthcare/5676511-ai-prescriptions-utah-doctronic/) | [MobiHealthNews](https://www.mobihealthnews.com/news/utah-allows-ai-renew-prescription-drugs-autonomously)

Utah became the **first U.S. state** to authorize an AI system to autonomously renew drug prescriptions through a regulatory sandbox program. The state partnered with startup **Doctronic**, whose AI platform allows patients with chronic conditions to renew 190 eligible medications without a physician visit — verified via selfie, photo ID, and a symptom chatbot. Excluded are painkillers, injectables, and ADHD medications; initial prescriptions still require a human doctor. The program, launched in January 2026, continues to expand amid ongoing debate about FDA oversight and patient safety.
