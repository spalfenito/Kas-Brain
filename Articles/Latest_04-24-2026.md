## 1. OpenAI Launches GPT-5.5 with Major Intelligence Leap
**Source:** [LLM Stats / AI News — April 2026](https://llm-stats.com/ai-news)

OpenAI released GPT-5.5, a new flagship model that matches GPT-5.4's per-token latency in real-world serving while delivering significantly higher intelligence. The model is priced at $5/1M input tokens and $30/1M output tokens — double GPT-5.4's pricing. A GPT-5.5 Pro tier is also available at $30/1M input and $180/1M output tokens. **Source:** [TechStartups — Top Tech News, April 23–24, 2026](https://techstartups.com/2026/04/23/top-tech-news-today-april-23-2026/)

## 4. Snap Cuts 1,000 Jobs, Cites AI Efficiency Gains
**Source:** [TechCrunch — Artificial Intelligence](https://techcrunch.com/category/artificial-intelligence/)

Snap CEO Evan Spiegel announced the layoff of approximately 1,000 employees and the closure of over 300 open roles, directly attributing the cuts to "rapid advancements in artificial intelligence" that allow smaller teams to achieve equivalent output. The announcement underscores a growing industry trend of AI-driven workforce restructuring at major tech firms, as generative AI tools increasingly automate tasks previously requiring large teams.

## 5. Geoffrey Hinton Urges Caution as ICLR 2026 Kicks Off in Rio
**Source:** [UN News — Time to Apply the Brakes to Runaway AI](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/04/1167361)

Nobel laureate and AI pioneer Geoffrey Hinton spoke at the Digital World Conference: AI for Social Development, warning that the rapid pace of AI advancement must be steered more carefully to serve societies rather than undermine them.
