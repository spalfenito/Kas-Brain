## 2. Harvard Study: OpenAI Model Outperforms ER Doctors at Diagnosis

**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/03/in-harvard-study-ai-offered-more-accurate-diagnoses-than-emergency-room-doctors/)

A study published in *Science* by researchers at Harvard Medical School and Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center found that OpenAI's o1 reasoning model correctly diagnosed patients in 67% of emergency room triage cases, compared to 55% and 50% for two experienced physicians working from the same electronic health records. The model was tested across hundreds of clinical cases ranging from published vignettes to live ER patients. Authors caution the AI operated on text alone and do not advocate replacing clinicians, noting that real-world care involves images, sounds, and nonverbal cues not captured in records.

**Source:** [Google Cloud Blog](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/gemini-3-1-flash-lite-is-now-generally-available)
