## 3. Snap Lays Off 16% of Workforce as AI Writes 65% of Its Code

Snap CEO Evan Spiegel announced the layoff of approximately 1,000 employees — 16% of its full-time workforce — and closure of over 300 open roles, citing AI-driven efficiency gains. The company says 65% of its codebase is now generated by AI, enabling smaller teams to maintain the same output. The cuts are expected to reduce Snap's annualized cost base by over $500 million by H2 2026. Spiegel also warned that tech leaders "vastly underestimate the societal pushback" to AI displacement, even as the restructuring is driven directly by AI adoption.
